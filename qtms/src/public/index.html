How It Works

This system models information transmission "back in time" using quantum delayed choice experiments. Each bit of information is encoded as a separate quantum experiment, where the choice of measurement type (presence or absence of the second beam splitter) determines the bit value.

In the classic delayed choice experiment, the decision about the measurement method is made after the particle has "decided" its path. This demonstrates the strange property of quantum mechanics: future events can influence past ones.

In our model, we use hidden parameters to control this process and ensure predictable information transmission. You can adjust these parameters and observe how they affect transmission accuracy.
